What is Color-Blindness?

Color-blidness is when you can't see colors in a normal, or the more popular way. When a person has color-blindness, they aren't able to distinguish colors between each other.

Accessibility Color Wheel

The Accessibility Color Wheel is an online tool that helps you pick colors that are easier for people with color-blindness to see. This tool explains three types of colr blindness: Deuteranope- insensitivity to green, Protanope- insensitvity to red, and Tritanope- insensitivty to blue (very rare).

Color Labratory

The Color Labratory allows you to simulate the colors that people with color-blindness will see. It also allows you to pick colors to see how they look together and if you can see them together.

Color-Blind Web Page Filter

The Colorblind Web Page Filter allows you to insert the URL of your website and it checks to see if a person with color-blindness would be able to access your website.

Color-Blindness Facts

Color-blindness affects 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women. The most common versions of color-blindess are pople who cannot distinguish red and green colors.
